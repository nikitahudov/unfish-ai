## Introduction

In Module 3.2, you learned about value betting—betting to get called by worse hands. But what about when you don't have a made hand? What about when you have a draw?

This is where the **semi-bluff** comes in—one of the most powerful plays in poker.

<KeyConcept title="What Is a Semi-Bluff?">
A **semi-bluff** is a bet or raise made with a drawing hand that isn't currently the best hand but has equity to improve.

Unlike a pure bluff (where you have no chance to win if called), a semi-bluff has **two ways to win**.
</KeyConcept>

Semi-bluffs are the perfect blend of aggression and equity. You're applying pressure while maintaining a backup plan. When executed correctly, they're among the most profitable plays in poker.

---

## The Two Ways to Win

This is the core principle of semi-bluffing:

<KeyConcept title="Two Ways to Win">
**Way 1: Opponent Folds** — You win the pot immediately.

**Way 2: Opponent Calls** — You can improve to the best hand on future streets.

A pure bluff only has Way 1. A value bet relies on Way 2 working in your favor. A semi-bluff has BOTH.
</KeyConcept>

### Why This Is So Powerful

Imagine you have a flush draw on the flop (9 outs, ~35% to hit by the river).

**If you just call:**
- You win when you hit (~35%)
- You lose when you miss (~65%)

**If you semi-bluff:**
- You win when opponent folds (let's say 40%)
- You win when you're called AND hit (60% × 35% = 21%)
- You lose when called AND miss (60% × 65% = 39%)
- **Total win rate: 61%**

Semi-bluffing transforms a 35% equity hand into a 61% winning play.

## Semi-Bluff Math

Let's formalize when semi-bluffs are profitable:

<KeyConcept variant="formula" title="Semi-Bluff EV Formula">
**EV = (Fold% × Pot) + (Call% × (Equity × New Pot - Bet))**

Where:
- Fold% = How often opponent folds
- Pot = Current pot size
- Call% = How often opponent calls
- Equity = Your chance to win when called
- New Pot = Pot after opponent calls
- Bet = Your bet size
</KeyConcept>

### Example Calculation

**Situation:**
- Pot: $100
- Your bet: $75
- Opponent folds: 40%
- Your equity when called: 35%

**EV = (0.40 × $100) + (0.60 × (0.35 × $250 - $75))**
**EV = $40 + (0.60 × ($87.50 - $75))**
**EV = $40 + (0.60 × $12.50)**
**EV = $40 + $7.50**
**EV = +$47.50**

This semi-bluff is highly profitable!

## Semi-Bluff Sizing

Your bet size affects both fold equity and pot odds:

### Standard Semi-Bluff Sizing

**50-75% pot** is typical for semi-bluffs:
- Large enough to generate folds
- Small enough that you're not risking too much
- Creates reasonable pot odds for the next street

### All-In Semi-Bluffs

When stack-to-pot ratio is right (around 1:1 or less), shoving can be correct:
- Maximum fold equity
- Realize all your equity if called
- No more decisions—simplifies the hand

### Small Semi-Bluffs (Probing)

Sometimes a small bet (25-40% pot) works:
- Tests opponent's hand strength
- Builds the pot for later
- Good on turns when draws brick

### The Check-Raise Semi-Bluff

Out of position, the check-raise is your most powerful semi-bluffing weapon.

<KeyConcept title="Check-Raise Semi-Bluff">
**How it works:** Check to the aggressor, they bet, you raise.

**Why it's powerful:**
- Represents extreme strength
- Puts maximum pressure on opponent
- Can take down pots immediately
- Sets up big turn/river bets

**Best hands:** Combo draws, flush draws with overcards, pair + flush draw
</KeyConcept>

## When NOT to Semi-Bluff

Semi-bluffs aren't always correct. Avoid them in these situations:

### Against Calling Stations

If your opponent never folds, you have no fold equity. Your "semi-bluff" becomes just a bet with equity—which might still be okay, but you're not getting the full benefit.

### With Weak Draws

Gutshots and backdoor draws alone don't have enough equity. You need significant fold equity to compensate, and you're better off taking a free card.

### In Multiway Pots

With multiple opponents, someone likely has a hand. Semi-bluffs lose effectiveness when you need everyone to fold.

### When You Have Showdown Value

If you have a medium-strength hand that can win at showdown (like bottom pair with a flush draw), sometimes checking is better than turning your hand into a bluff.

## Common Semi-Bluff Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Semi-Bluffing Without Enough Equity

A gutshot isn't a semi-bluff—it's a bluff with a tiny chance of hitting. You need real equity (8+ outs) to semi-bluff effectively.

### Mistake 2: Over-Aggression With Draws

Bombing every draw for three streets will get exploited. Mix in some calls and checks to remain unpredictable.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Opponent Tendencies

Semi-bluffs work against players who fold. Against calling stations, just see your cards cheaply or bet for thin value when you hit.

### Mistake 4: Not Considering Stack Depth

Short stacks change the math. With 15bb, you can't semi-bluff small—it's shove or fold. Deep stacks give more flexibility.

### Mistake 5: Semi-Bluffing Dry Boards

On K♠7♦2♣, there are few draws. "Semi-bluffing" with a gutshot here is really just bluffing—you need a wet board to have meaningful draws.

## Advanced: Balancing Semi-Bluffs

Good players don't just semi-bluff draws—they balance their ranges.

### What This Means

When you bet a draw-heavy board, you should have:
- **Value hands:** Sets, two pair, overpairs
- **Semi-bluffs:** Flush draws, straight draws
- **Some checks:** Trap hands, weaker draws

This balance makes you harder to play against. Opponents can't simply raise you off every draw.

### The Concept of Polarization

On some boards, you want a **polarized** strategy:
- Bet strong hands (value)
- Bet strong draws (semi-bluffs)
- Check medium hands

This creates a betting range of "nutted hands or draws" that pressures opponents effectively.

## Summary

<KeyConcept title="Key Takeaways">
1. **Two ways to win** — Opponent folds OR you improve. This is the power of semi-bluffing.
2. **Need real equity** — 8+ outs (flush draws, OESDs, combo draws). Gutshots alone aren't enough.
3. **Fold equity matters** — Semi-bluffs work best against players who can fold.
4. **Position affects strategy** — Bet/raise in position; check-raise out of position.
5. **Avoid multiway and calling stations** — You need opponents to fold.
6. **Balance your range** — Don't semi-bluff every draw; mix in calls and checks.
</KeyConcept>

Semi-bluffing is what makes draws exciting and profitable. Instead of passively hoping to hit, you're taking control of the pot with built-in equity. Master this skill, and you'll extract value from situations where passive players break even at best.
